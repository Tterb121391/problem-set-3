The Five Most Important Rules of Ultimate

Since Ultimate frisbee is a sport, it obviously has its own set of rules to make sure that the game stays somewhat orderly and doesn't get out of control. Here are the five most important tules of Ultimate listed from most important to least important:

  1. Physical contact that affects the play is not allowed. A foul occurs when non-incidental physical contact occurs. Non-incidental contact means contact that has affected the play. Picks and screens are also considered fouls.
  2. A player is only allowed to pivot once while holding the disc. Moving the other foot that is supposed to be fixed is considered a travel and a foul.
  3. If a player has both feet out or one foot in and one foot out when he/she catches the disc, then the ruling is out, and it is a turnover.
  4. If a player catches the disc inbounds then runs out of bounds, then he/she must come back in and tap the disc on the ground to continue the play. If a player catches the disc and runs into the end zone, that is considered a walk-in, and the player must bring the disc back onto the end zone line and tap the disc on the ground to continue the play.
  5. If a foul is called and contested by the other player, then the previous play must be redone. Otherwise, if the foul was uncontested, then the play resumes as usual.
