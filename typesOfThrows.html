There are many, many different types of throws that are usable in a legitimate Ultimate match, but if you want to be just a casual player, then there are only three basic throws to learn. The flick of the wrist is key in learning to throw, and without it, your throws will just wobble and drop after a very short distance.

The Backhand

The backhand is usually the first type of throw the average person learns. Grip the frisbee with the thumb on top and the other four fingers supporting on the bottom. Use one leg to pivot across the other leg and throw. For righties, the disc is held with the right hand, and the right foot is used as the pivot foot (left leg stays put), and vice versa for lefties.

The Forehand

The forehand is thrown on the other side of the body, but the pivot foot stays the same. Grip the frisbee with the thumb on top, the pointer and middle fingers supporting on the bottom, and the ring and pinky fingers relaxed and not holding any part of the frisbee. Pivot outwards from the other leg and throw.

The Hammer

The hammer is a more advanced throw than the other two, but it is the most basic of the upside down throws. The grip of throwing a hammer is the same grip as throwing a forehand, but you are now tilting it diagonally above your head and throwing it from there. The pivot for throwing a hammer is the same as the pivot for throwing a backhand.
